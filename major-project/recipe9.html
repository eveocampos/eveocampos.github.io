Bunuelos

Ingredients

  • 6-8 Uncooked Tortillas, (make your own or alternatively, find uncooked tortillas in your supermarket)
  • 1 Cup Vegtable oil (for frying tortillas)
  • 3/4 Cup of Sugar
  • 1-2 tsp Ground Cinnamon
  • If making your own tortillas: 2 Cups All-purpose Flour / 1 tsp salt / 6.5oz of Hot Water / 1/8 Cup Vegetable oil.

Directions

  1. Begin by combining sugar and cinnamon, mix well. Set aside to sprinkle over your tortillas.
  2. If making your own tortillas: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Then, add the water and vegetable oil and using your hands, mix together until a soft dough forms (all the liquid should be absorbed). If the dough is too sticky, add more flour 1 tablespoon at a time. The dough should be soft and Playdoh-like in texture, but not sticky. Transfer the dough onto your work surface and knead it about 10 times until it becomes smooth all over. You will then form 6 balls of dough, slightly flatten each ball with your hand and roll out to roughly 8-inch discs. (Roll each one out one at time to allow time to cook each one before rolling out the next. This will prevent the dough from sticking to the work surface.)
  3. If buying uncooked tortillas from the store you are ready to fry.
  4. To begin: Line a large plate with paper towels. Set aside.
  5. Heat oil in a medium frying pan. Once hot, carefully place one tortilla into oil.
  6. Cook on each side until golden brown, approx. 5 min total.
  7. Carefully remove from oil and place on paper lined plate. Generously sprinkle each side of tortilla with cinnamon and sugar mixture.
  8. Repeat until all tortillas are done. Place remaining cinnamon and sugar mixture in a bowl for extra sprinkling.
  9. Serve (pairs perfectly with "Mexican Hot Cholocate") and enjoy!
