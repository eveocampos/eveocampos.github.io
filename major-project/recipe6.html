Chicken Fajitas

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless Chicken Breast, cut into strips
  • 1-2 Bell Peppers, cut into strips roughly the same size as your chicken (I use Red and Yellow peppers, but use what you like)
  • 1 Onion, cut into strips
  • 6-8 Tortillas (homemade or store bought)
  • 1 tsp of Garlic powder, Onion powder, Red Chili powder, Salt & Pepper to taste (you can add fajita seasoning instead, but I keep spices fairly simple as is traditional in our family)
  • 1 Tblsp Olive Oil
  • Toppings: Guacamole (can use the recipe "Guacamole"), Sourcream, Lime, anything else your heart desires (I use guacamole and sometimes sourcream)

Directions

  1. If you are making homemade tortillas, make these first: If making your own tortillas: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Then, add the water and vegetable oil and using your hands, mix together until a soft dough forms (all the liquid should be absorbed). If the dough is too sticky, add more flour 1 tablespoon at a time. The dough should be soft and Playdoh-like in texture, but not sticky. Transfer the dough onto your work surface and knead it about 10 times until it becomes smooth all over. You will then form 6 balls of dough, slightly flatten each ball with your hand and roll out to roughly 8-inch discs. (Roll each one out one at time to allow time to cook each one before rolling out the next. This will prevent the dough from sticking to the work surface.)
  2. To Cook your tortillas (wheather homemade or store bought): Heat a non-stick pan over medium high heat (no oil is necessary). Once hot, place one tortilla in pan and allow to cook on each side until tortilla starts to get golden brown spots, approx. 1 min each side. When done, place tortillas in foil to keep warm while you make your fajitas.
  3. To a large pan, add olive oil and heat over medium high heat. Once heated, add your chicken, and spices and cook until chicken is cooked through,but not charred (about 5-6 minutes).
  4. Next, add in all your bellpeppers and onions and continue cooking until chicken is slightly charred and veggies are soft but not mushy (about 5 min more). (Alternatively, you can fire up the grill and grill you chicken (marinated in spices) and veggies)
  5. When cooked, remove from heat and serve with tortillas and toppings.
  6. This meal goes perfectly with Mexican rice, Refried beans, Salsa Quemada, Guacamole, and Margaritas or Palomas! Serve and enjoy!
