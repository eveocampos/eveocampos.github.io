The

Columbian Mammoth

Scroll Down

Columbian Mammoths roamed eastern Washington throughout the Pleistocene Epoch (the last Ice Age).

Cataclysmic Ice Age floods (such as those released from Glacial Lake Missoula) poured across Eastern Washington, sweeping many mammoths and other creatures to their deaths.

In November 1999, mammoth bones were discovered in a quarry south of Kennewick, Washington. It was established that the mammoth bones were located in Ice Age Flood deposits.

Formal excavation of the site began in September, 2010.

By the end of the 2016 dig season, we had collected nearly 700 specimens, including 97 mammoth bones or bone fragments.

Excavation of the site continues two weekends per month from March through October.
